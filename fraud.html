Common Property Frauds

  • Fake property listings with attractive but unreal prices.
  • Scammers asking for advance payments without legal documents.
  • Forged property ownership papers or duplicate selling.
  • Rental frauds with fake landlords demanding security deposits.
  • Fraudulent brokers charging hidden commissions.

How to Identify Frauds

  • Unverified sellers avoiding face-to-face meetings.
  • No official documentation (sale deed, registry, NOC).
  • Pressuring buyers for urgent decisions.
  • Payment requests only via cash or personal accounts.

Safety Tips

  • Always verify property ownership in government records.
  • Never pay advance without written agreements.
  • Use legal advisors for document verification.
  • Demand official receipts for every payment.
  • Work with trusted and registered real estate companies.
